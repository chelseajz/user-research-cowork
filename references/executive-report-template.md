# Executive Report Template

Use this when the audience includes executives, GMs, research leads, or multi-function leadership.

## Page 1: Decision summary

Keep this to one page or one screen.

### Recommended structure

```markdown
# [Research title]

## What decision this supports
- [Decision 1]
- [Decision 2]

## What we know
- [Finding 1 with strongest evidence]
- [Finding 2 with strongest evidence]
- [Finding 3 with strongest evidence]

## What we do not know yet
- [Key uncertainty 1]
- [Key uncertainty 2]

## What we recommend now
- P0: [Action]
- P1: [Action]

## Risk if misread
- [Most important caveat or sampling constraint]
```

## Full report body

After the decision summary, use this sequence:

1. Business context
2. Research scope and questions
3. Method and sample
4. Executive findings
5. Segment detail or theme detail
6. Recommendations and prioritization
7. Limitations
8. Open questions
9. Appendix

For substantial leadership-facing research, prefer two companion outputs:
- a formal main report that carries the full evidence chain
- a shorter meeting version that compresses the same conclusions for fast discussion

The formal main report should still be readable front-to-back without requiring readers to open a separate `detailed findings` document.

## Executive-writing rules

- Lead with the implication, then support it.
- Reduce jargon unless the audience already uses it.
- Do not present more than 5 top findings in the summary.
- Every recommendation should answer: what should a VP or head of product do differently after reading this?
- Name where confidence is limited so leadership does not over-operate on weak signals.
- Keep the summary plainspoken and specific; avoid consultant-sounding phrasing.
- Even in executive mode, keep at least a few memorable user lines or scene snippets in the body so the report does not turn bloodless.
- When a conclusion has numeric support, lead with the number and then interpret it.
- Use rich-doc formatting intentionally: callout blocks for key judgments, tables for comparison, and quotes for user reality.
- If charts are shown in Feishu or a similar tool, place them near the relevant section rather than dumping them all at the end.

## Recommendation table

Suggested format:

| Priority | Action | Supported by | Expected impact | Confidence | Owner |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| P0 | [action] | [finding ids] | [outcome] | [H/M/L] | [team] |

## Common executive-report failures

- Buried answer: the conclusion appears too late
- Excessive evidence dump without interpretation
- Recommendations are not prioritized
- Caveats exist but are impossible to notice
- Findings are interesting but not decision-relevant
- Summary sounds polished but generic, with too little user reality
- Findings are over-compressed and lose the concrete situation behind the behavior
